Creamy Pasta

A comforting, creamy pasta dish packed with fresh vegetables and flavor, perfect for a quick dinner or cozy weekend meal. This vegetarian-friendly recipe uses a garlic cream sauce and colorful seasonal veggies.

Ingredients

  • 250g pasta (spaghetti, penne, tagliatelle)
  • 2 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 bell pepper (sliced)
  • 250g mushrooms (sliced)
  • 100g spinach (fresh)
  • 50g cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • 200ml heavy cream or plant-based cream
  • 50g grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs or basil

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta:
    Bring a large pot of water(2-3L) to a boil. Cook pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside

  2. Saute vegetables:
    In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic; cook until soft and fragrant(about 2 minutes). Add mushrooms and bell pepper; cook for 5-7 minutes until tender. Add cherry tomatoes and spinach, cooking until spinach wilts.

  3. Make the sauce:
    Reduce heat to low. Pour in cream and stir in Parmesan cheese and herbs. Season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer gently for 3-4 minutes until slightly thickened. Add a splash of pasta water if it becomes too thick.

  4. Combine:
    Add the cooked pasta into the pan and toss until everything is well coated. Adjust seasoning if needed.

  5. Serve:
    Plate up and sprinkle with fresh herbs and extra cheese or nutritonal yeast. Enjoy warm!

Pesto Pasta

A simple, aromatic pasta dish tossed with fresh basil pesto, crunchy pine nuts, and grated cheese. Light yet satisfying, it's a perfect weeknight dinner or summer lunch — and comes together in minutes.

Ingredients

  • 250g pasta (e.g. spaghetti, fusilli, or farfalle)
  • 1 small courgette (zucchini), diced or thinly sliced
  • 100g cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • Optional: 50g green beans or peas (lightly steamed)
  • 30g fresh basil leaves (about 2 packed cups)
  • 30g pine nuts (or walnuts)
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 50g Parmesan or vegetarian hard cheese (grated)

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta:
    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve ¼ cup of pasta water, then drain.

  2. Prepare the vegetables:
    While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté the courgette and cherry tomatoes for 5–7 minutes until tender and lightly golden. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Make the pesto:
    In a food processor or blender, combine basil, pine nuts, garlic, Parmesan, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt. Blend while slowly streaming in olive oil until smooth. Adjust consistency with a splash of water or more oil if needed.
  4. Combine everything:
    Return the drained pasta to the pot or a large bowl. Stir in the pesto and cooked vegetables. Add a splash of pasta water to help coat the pasta if necessary.

  5. Serve:
    Plate and top with fresh basil, extra cheese, and pine nuts. Serve warm or cold!

Tomato Pasta

A timeless favorite, this tomato pasta features a rich and savory homemade tomato sauce made with garlic, herbs, and ripe tomatoes. Perfect for a quick weekday meal, and easy to customize with your favorite vegetables or toppings.

Ingredients

  • 250g pasta (e.g. penne, spaghetti, or rigatoni)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 400g canned chopped tomatoes (or passata)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano or Italian herbs
  • 1 small courgette (zucchini), diced
  • 1 handful fresh spinach or basil leaves

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve about ½ cup of pasta water, then drain and set aside.

  2. Make the tomato sauce:
    In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic for 2–3 minutes until softened. Add the tomato paste and cook for 1 minute to deepen the flavor.

  3. Add tomatoes and season:
    Pour in the canned tomatoes. Stir in sugar, herbs, salt, and pepper. Simmer uncovered for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens. Add any optional veggies (like cherry tomatoes or courgette) during the simmering process.

  4. Combine:
    Stir the cooked pasta into the sauce. If needed, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce and coat the pasta evenly.

  5. Serve:
    Top with fresh basil, grated cheese, or a drizzle of olive oil. Serve hot with crusty bread or a green salad.
